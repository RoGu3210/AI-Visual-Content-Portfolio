## Monday — Post 1 · `post-1.png` (What ThreadPup is)
**Custom tees, printed on demand. No minimums, no leftover stock.**

Most apparel brands gamble on inventory — print 500 shirts and hope they sell.
ThreadPup flips that: you design it, we print it *after* it's ordered, and it
ships in days.

Three things make it click:
🎨 Design your own — live editor, instant preview
🖨️ Printed on demand — zero dead stock
🚚 Shipped to the door — tracked end to end

This week I'm breaking down how the whole thing works — product, order flow,
tech stack, customer journey, and the economics. Follow along. 👇

## Tuesday — Post 2 · `post-2.png` (How an order flows)
**What actually happens in the 3 seconds after you click "Buy"?**

A lot. Here's the order's journey through the system, status by status:

🛒 Order placed → 💳 Stripe charges the card → 🖨️ the print shop produces &
QCs it → 📦 it's packed with a tracking number → 🚚 delivered.

Every transition updates one `status` field and fires an email, so the customer
always knows where their shirt is. Boring-on-purpose reliability is a feature.

How do you keep customers informed during fulfillment? 👇

#ecommerce #orderfulfillment #stripe #buildinpublic #customerexperience

## Wednesday — Post 3 · `post-3.png` (The tech stack)
**The whole ThreadPup stack on one slide.**

No monolith, no servers to babysit:

🔵 Customer-facing — Next.js on Vercel
🟢 Backend & fulfillment — Supabase, a Fulfillment API, the Print Shop
🟠 Third-party — Stripe (payments), Resend (email)

Serverless, type-safe, and cheap to run until you actually have orders. That's
the point of print-on-demand: keep fixed costs near zero.

What would you add or swap? 👇

#nextjs #supabase #stripe #techstack #serverless

## Thursday — Post 4 · `post-4.png` (The customer journey)
**Turning a first-time buyer into a regular.**

The product is shirts. The actual product is the *journey*:

🔍 Discover → 🎨 Customize → 🛒 Buy → 📦 Receive → 🔁 Repeat

The whole funnel is designed so the fifth step is the easy one — a great unboxing
and one-click reorders do more for retention than any ad. Acquisition gets you the
first order; experience gets you the next ten.

Where do most stores lose people in this flow? 👇
