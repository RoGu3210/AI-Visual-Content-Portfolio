## Script (with timecodes)

**[0:00 — HOOK · frame-01]**
You've probably typed something into an AI, gotten a vague, generic answer back,
and figured the tool just wasn't that smart. Here's the twist: most of the time
the model is fine — it's the *prompt* that's letting you down. In the next three
minutes I'll show you four quick fixes — four simple techniques the pros use — to
get sharp, reliable answers every single time.

**[0:28 — PROBLEM · frame-02]**
First, the core problem. AI runs on one simple rule: vague in, vague out. If you
ask "write something about marketing," the model has to guess your topic, your
audience, your format, your length — and it guesses *average*, because average
is the safest bet. You didn't get a bad model. You handed it a blurry photo and
asked it to describe the details. Fix the input, and the output snaps into focus.

**[1:00 — CONCEPT · frame-03]**
So here are the four techniques. **One: Zero-shot** — just ask clearly and add
context: who it's for, the format, the constraints. **Two: Few-shot** — show one
or two examples of what "good" looks like, and the model matches the pattern.
**Three: Chain-of-thought** — tell it to think step by step before answering, so
it reasons instead of blurting. **Four: Role** — assign it a persona: "You are a
senior copywriter." Role sets the vocabulary, the standards, and the tone.

**[1:50 — EXAMPLE · frame-04]**
Let's see chain-of-thought in action. Ask plainly, "Maya has 7 apples and buys
4 more — how many?" and a rushed model might just spit out a number. Now add four
words: "think step by step." Step one: start with 7. Step two: add the 4 she
buys. Step three: 7 plus 4 equals 11. Same question, but now the reasoning is
visible — so it's correct, and you can actually check the work.

**[2:35 — SUMMARY · frame-05]**
Quick recap. Add context. Show an example. Ask it to reason step by step. Give it
a role. That's it — four moves that work in any AI tool you already use. None of
this requires code or special access; it's just clearer instructions. Try it on
your next prompt — rewrite one request using even one of these, and watch the
answer get noticeably better. If this helped, subscribe for more. See you next time.
